Edusave Awards

Ministry of Education (MOE) established the Education Endowment Fund Scheme, commonly known as the Edusave Scheme, in January 1993, to maximise and equalise educational opportunities for all Singaporeans, enhance the quality of education in Singapore and motivate students to excel academically.
The Edusave awards, funded by the Education Endowment Fund, are given out yearly to students who have achieved excellence in academic and non-academic areas through the Edusave awards listed below.


Calculation Per Capita Income (PCI)

PCI = Total Gross Monthly Household Income (GHI) ÷ Number of Family Members in the Household (including student)


Edusave Merit Bursary (EMB)


Edusave Merit Bursary (EMB) is one of the Edusave awards given out yearly to students who have achieved excellence in academic areas.

Eligibility

  • Singapore Citizen
  • Full-time Diploma student or Polytechnic Foundation Programme# student
  • Top 25% of each level & course
  • GHI ≤ $7,500, or PCI ≤ $1,875
  • Good conduct

    • Polytechnic Foundation Programme – Top 25% of Science & Technology (S&T) courses and Top 25% of Non-S&T courses.

    Value of Award

  • Edusave Merit Bursary is valued at $500 per academic year.


    • Edusave Good Progress Award (GPA)


    Edusave Good Progress Award (GPA) is one of the Edusave awards given out yearly to students who have improved their academic grades.

    Eligibility

  • Singapore Citizen
  • Full-time Diploma student in second or third year of study
  • Top 10% of each level & course, based on good progress (improvement)
  • Must not be in receipt of the Edusave Merit Bursary
  • Good conduct

    • Value of Award

  • Edusave Good Progress Award is valued at $400 per academic year.


    • Edusave Skills Award (ESA)


    Edusave Skills Award (ESA) is one of the Edusave awards given out yearly to students who have demonstrated excellent professional and soft skills throughout their course of study, based on their performance in curriculum components and/or achievements in competitions, conferences or other platforms that require the application of course-specific skills.

    Eligibility

  • Singapore Citizen
  • Full-time Diploma student in second or third year of study
  • Up to 5% (for Year 2) and 10% (for Year 3) of Singapore Citizens from each course in the graduating cohort
  • Nominated by School
  • Good conduct
  • Demonstrated mastery of diploma-specific skills and soft skills in areas relevant to course of study, based on:
    • Performance during internship and Final Year Projects;
    • Participation in diploma-related industry exposure opportunities;
    • Participation in skills-related service learning projects;
    • Achievements at diploma-related competitions and conferences

    • Value of Award

  • Edusave Skills Award is valued at $500 per academic year.
